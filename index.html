Common questions

Why FY2025 and not an average?
FY2023 includes a one-off $124K grant and Higher Ground was half its current size. Averaging in those years sets an artificially easy target. FY2025 reflects the business as it stands today.
What is the difference between the two toggle views?
Commissions and advances combined includes both earned commissions and artist advances, with reimbursements excluded. Commissions only further strips out advances ($429K in FY2025), leaving only earned commission income for Higher Ground. Comparing the two helps determine a fair baseline figure.
Why do advances matter?
Advances are money paid to artists upfront against future earnings. They inflate trading income but are recoupable, not purely earned revenue. Separating them gives a cleaner picture of sustainable income growth. The updated P&L now breaks these out as a separate line item.
Should we update the baseline once FY2026 closes?
Yes. FY2026 ends 31 March. Once final figures are in, confirm whether FY2025 or FY2026 is the fairer starting point before the hire's performance period begins.
Is 10% all-or-nothing too risky?
Revenue swings year to year. A tiered approach (5% = $10K, 10% = $20K) would reward incremental growth and keep motivation through slower periods. Worth discussing.
